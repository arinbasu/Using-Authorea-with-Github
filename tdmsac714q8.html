The above diagram shows that in the association between X and Y, Z is a confounding variable. As you can see, Z is associated with both X and Y, but Z does not come in a causal connection that links X and Y. For example, in Nordic countries, Eero Pukkala and colleagues studied airline pilot who were considered to be at high risk of developing  skin cancer \cite{Pukkala_2002}
