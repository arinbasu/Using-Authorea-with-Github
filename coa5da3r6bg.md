In the causal pie models, each slice of the pie is referred to as a component cause; if several sufficient causal models are developed for the same phenomenon, then the most common component cause or the component cause that were to be found in all sufficient causal models would be referred to as a necessary cause. A cause does not have to be sufficient or necessary for cause to occur. As an example, imagine an old woman slipping on a road of ice and breaks her hip. What was the cause of her hip fracture?
